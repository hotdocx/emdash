• After the second pass, I think your correction is basically right, but it forces a sharper split than the current report has. My
  earlier objection was really against the current report’s transportd_sec E x u for arbitrary E : Catd Z, not against making Catd Z
  core-indexed.

  If Catd Z is definitionally or morally:

  Functor (Core_cat Z) Cat_cat

  then it is a HoTT/displayed-type-family layer. It has transport along paths/equivalences in Obj Z; it does not need, and should not
  pretend to have, transport along every directed arrow of Z.

  Then when we want directed opfibration behavior, we use:

  M : Functor Z Cat_cat

  and a restriction/forgetful bridge:

  Fibration_cov_catd M : Catd Z

  morally:

  M ∘ Core_incl_func Z : Functor (Core_cat Z) Cat_cat

  That preserves the semantic meaning of Catd. So yes: my previous “Catd must be opfibration-like” statement is too strong if we adopt
  this split.

  The consequence is that the current report must be revised more deeply than I had written. In particular:

  transportd_sec E x u

  should not be primitive for arbitrary E : Catd Z under the core-family interpretation. It should become something like:

  transport_cov_sec M x u

  for M : Functor Z Cat_cat, or for a separate directed-family interface if we later introduce one. Its beta rule is then honest:

  transport_cov_sec M x u [y, f] ↪ fib_cov_tapp0_fapp0 M f u

  For arbitrary E : Catd Z, the available generic transport is only core/path transport, not directed transport over f : Hom_cat Z x y.

  This also means Sigma_cat E changes meaning. For core-indexed E, Sigma_cat E is the HoTT-style dependent sum over equality/core paths.
  Its homs should look like “base path plus fibre hom after path transport.” It is not the full directed Grothendieck total over all
  arrows of Z.

  So if we want the usual directed Grothendieck hom formula:

  Hom_{∫M}((x,u),(y,v))
    = Σ(f : Hom_Z x y), Hom_{M y}(M(f)u, v)

  then the total should be a directed construction attached to M, for example Total_cov_cat M / Groth_cat M, not generically Sigma_cat
  (Fibration_cov_catd M) unless we deliberately keep Fibration_cov_catd M as a tagged constructor with hidden directed data. I think the
  tagged route is less clean because it makes operations on Catd depend on data that the core-family semantics has forgotten.

  Where the existing edge_at_func idea still survives: Edge_catd Z x can be a core-indexed family with fibre

  Fibre_cat (Edge_catd Z x) y ≡ Op_cat (Hom_cat Z x y)

  because it only needs to vary functorially along core changes of y. The inclusion

  piapp0 (sigma_intro_functord (Edge_catd Z x)) y

  is still a good way to package “fix endpoint y, include arrows x -> y into the edge context.” But the section assigning f ↦ M(f)u is
  no longer generic in E; it is generic in directed M.

  So my updated recommendation is:

  - Make Catd Z the core/internal family layer.
  - Keep Functor Z Cat_cat as the directed opfibration layer.
  - Let Fibration_cov_catd be restriction from directed families to core families.
  - Split Sigma_cat from directed Grothendieck total.
  - Rewrite the Sigma-hom plan so the directed Sigma/Groth hom formula is built for M : Functor Z Cat_cat, while the generic Sigma_cat E
    formula is the HoTT/core-path Sigma formula.
  - Keep Functor_catd, Pi_cat, Catd_catd, Transf_catd as core-family constructions; they are still useful and more principled in this
    reading.

  The main thing I would not keep from the current report is “directed displayed transport for general Catd.” That is exactly the
  semantic leak your clarification avoids.

• That clarification resolves the confusion: I was still treating Sigma_cat as an operation attached specifically to old Catd Z. In the
  redesign you are describing, Sigma_cat should be an operation on any Cat-valued functor E : K -> Cat_cat; then the directed
  Grothendieck total is just Sigma_cat M for M : Z -> Cat_cat, and the HoTT/core Sigma is Sigma_cat E for E : Core_cat Z -> Cat_cat.

  I think that is the better architecture.

  The foundation should become something like:

  Fam K := Functor K Cat_cat

  Catd Z := Fam (Core_cat Z)

  Then:

  Fibre_cat E k      := fapp0 E k
  Pullback_fam E F   := E ∘ F
  Const_fam K A      := constant functor K -> Cat_cat at A
  Sigma_cat E        := Grothendieck/dependent sum of E : Fam K
  Pi_cat E           := category of sections of E

  Under this reading, Fibration_cov_catd M becomes non-fundamental:

  Fibration_cov_catd M := Pullback_fam M (Core_incl_func Z)

  or more explicitly, restriction of M : Z -> Cat_cat to Core_cat Z.

  So yes, my proposed separate Total_cov_cat M was unnecessary if we generalize Sigma_cat to all K -> Cat_cat. The right split is not
  “core Sigma vs directed total”; it is one Sigma_cat over a general index category K, with different behavior depending on whether K =
  Z or K = Core_cat Z.

  For ω-iteration, the recovery is:

  E : Fam K
  Sigma_cat E : Cat

  and now the next layer can be indexed over the ordinary category Sigma_cat E:

  Fam (Sigma_cat E)          -- directed families over the total
  Catd (Sigma_cat E)         -- core/path families over the total

  So iteration is recovered by moving to the new base category Sigma_cat E, not by making Catd Z secretly carry all directed transport.

  A transformation between functors also still becomes the Grothendieck/displayed functor you cared about. If:

  η : Transf E D
  E D : Fam K

  then there should be a functor over K:

  sigma_map_func η : Functor (Sigma_cat E) (Sigma_cat D)

  with object computation:

  sigma_map_func η (k, u)
    ↪ (k, η_k(u))

  and projection law:

  Sigma_proj1_func D ∘ sigma_map_func η
    ↪ Sigma_proj1_func E

  This is exactly the old “a transformation between functors becomes a displayed functor between Grothendieck displayed categories,” but
  expressed as an ordinary functor between total categories over the same base. If we still want a category of such maps, it should be a
  slice/over category of functors over K, not Catd_cat K once Catd K means core-family.

  The main caveat is important: not every old Catd-indexed construction generalizes covariantly to arbitrary K.

  Sigma_cat, Pi_cat, Pullback, constants, and Sigma morphism-action work for arbitrary K -> Cat.

  But constructions like pointwise:

  Functor_catd E D : k ↦ Functor_cat (E k) (D k)
  Catd_catd E      : k ↦ Catd_cat (E k)

  are naturally safe over a core/groupoid base, because arrows are invertible and variance problems disappear. Over a general directed
  K, Functor(E k, D k) is not covariantly functorial in k unless the domain side has the opposite variance or extra adjoint/transport
  structure. Similarly, k ↦ Catd_cat(E k) is naturally contravariant in k, because a functor E k -> E k' gives pullback of families from
  E k' to E k.

  So the clean architecture is:

  Fam K = Functor K Cat_cat

  as the general directed family layer, with Sigma/Pi/pullback there;

  Catd Z = Fam (Core_cat Z)

  as the HoTT/core displayed-family layer, where pointwise internal constructions like Functor_catd, Catd_catd, Hom_catd, and
  Transf_catd are semantically better behaved because the base is core/groupoidal.

  That means the current report should pivot from “add directed transport to general Catd” to “promote Fam K as the primary indexed-
  family abstraction; define Catd as the core instance; move directed Sigma hom to generic Fam K.”
